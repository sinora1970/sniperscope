SniperScope Service

SniperScope only provides funny photography service . It only produces a photo like what is seen through a sniper scope using your device camera sensor data and stores this photo on your device storage  as two image files . The two files this application stores on your device storage are identical except one has a sniper scope overlay . They are produced from your device camera sensor information that captured by pressing Shot button in application panel. They are stored as two files with JPEG format in Android file system Pictures folder/Sniper subfolder with the names :

·       Sniper[yyyyMMdd_hhmmss]_org.jpg

·       Sniper[yyyyMMdd_hhmmss].jpg

When [yyyyMMdd_hhmmss] stands for photo capture time , yyyy as 4 digits year , MM as 2 digits month (01~12), dd as 2 digits day of month(01~31), hh as 2 digits hour of day (00~23) , mm as 2 digits minute (00~59), and ss as 2 digits second (00~59). The _org is for original photo without overlay .

SniperScope  simply receives camera sensor data , convert it to a graphic image object , does some graphical processes to put sniper overlay on it and converts and saves it as 2 JPEG files .

Versioning Policy

SniperScope has version code with three parts . Each part is separated by a DOT from other two parts . The first version is “1.0.0” and any minor versioning is shown in the right and middle parts . As mentioned above , minor versioning is only for debugging purposes and does not show a significant change in existing functions or any new function added . Major versioning on the other hand shows a new SniperScope app based on previous versions  with probably significant changes in existing functions or at least one or more new functions . For major versioning , the left part of version code will be changed . So next major version of SniperScope after version “1.0.0” and its subversions (for example “1.3.0”) have “2.0.0” version .
